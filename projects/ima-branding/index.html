IMA Branding

Nov 2022

This is a hypothetical logo and poster I designed for Interactive Media Arts (IMA), which is my department at NYU. I created this in my Design Fundamentals class taught by Professor Katherine Dillon.

Contribution: Graphic Design
Technologies: Figma

Logo

IMA doesn't currently have an official logo, so I started from scratch when brainstorming the design. The department is extremely interdisciplinary, focusing on the intersection of art and technology. Classes explore how digital interactivity can be used as a creative medium, in contexts like design, VR/AR, physical computing, web development, games, animation, digital fabrication, and more. In the initial brainstorming, I made a mind map of everything I associated with IMA, because I wanted to make sure the logo could tell a clear and accurate story about IMA's fundamental identity -- even to someone who has no prior knowledge of it.

From this, I started sketching rough ideas out on paper, coming up with as many ideas as possible before trying to narrow them down.

I took my two favorite concepts from these sketches and then developed them further in Figma. The first concept I liked was inspired by wooden tangram puzzles, as a nod to IMA's combination of logical thinking, problem-solving, and creativity. It also evokes themes of physicality and play. However, I realized that it slightly resembled blackletter-style typefaces and felt a little stiff and old-fashioned as a result. It is also quite complex and wouldn't translate well to the small dimensions of a browser icon, for instance. The second concept I liked was entirely composed of circles and squares, inspired by the geometric compositions of modern art and design movements like Bauhaus. I loved the playfulness, modernity, and simplicity of this one, but it wasn't as readable, especially when I tried adding color to it.

I iterated on these and finally came up with a logo that combined the tangram shapes of the first concept with the modernity and simplicity of the second.
